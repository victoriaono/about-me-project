About me

Life in Japan

I was born in a concrete jungle in Osaka, Japan. As an only child, my mother brought me up teaching me a variety of lessons. Ever since I was little, I immersed myself in arts: ballet from age 3 and piano from age 5. Ballet was, and is, my life. I went to lessons 6 times a week, performed in two recitals every year, and danced two different variations. Piano was more of a hobby to me, but I learned to play a waltz by Chopin, which became my ultimate favorite and a special piece of my life. I would also play duets with my mom, which remains one of my best memories.
My mom introduced me to western music, especially songs from the '80s and musicals. I would sing them in car rides not knowing the meanings of the lyrics. At 7 years old, I was obsessed with West Side Story.

While my mother taught me arts, my father read to me and taught me academics, especially math. I grew up loving math and science. We would all go to a science museum with a planetarium, or take a road trip to stargaze and name the stars or to observe a solar eclipse. By second grade, I was able to solve middle school level math and calculate large numbers in my head quickly. In 4th grade, I placed first in a countrywide academic test in Japan.

In 2009, my dream then came true. I received a puppy for my birthday present, and we named her Marie Antoinette. She was tiny, graceful but clumsy, and often jealous, but I adored her. Because I did not have any siblings, she was my sister and my treasure.

My mom had taught me a bit of English since I was little. Although I knew some simple vocabulary, that was not even close to survive a minute without help in the United States.

New York

Summer of 2011.
It was just me and my mom and my dog in the middle of Manhattan. Having lost my father and leaving my grandparents behind, we were lost ourselves.
I entered 5th grade and soon graduated. I was able to befriend people of different ethnicities that I had never seen before, and be a part of a school musical which I had never dreamed of. I still had my language barrier, but I advanced to an honors math class in middle school without knowing that I did. At the end of 8th grade, I received a medal for having the highest math average in class.


I knew that I had to go to a good high school. I learned of the SHSAT and decided that a specialized high school was where I needed to go. Many of my friends took it as well, and a lot of them were accepted into Bronx Science, including myself.
By this point, I had almost no trouble with English. Most of my friends and teachers did not even realize that I was a non-native speaker.
At Bronx Science, I joined the speech and debate team. I was a quarterfinalist at a tournament at University of Pennsylvania last year.

Future

Five and a half years have passed since I came to New York. I still need development in my writing, and there are many words that I still do not understand when I am reading a text in English or history class.
But I believe that, because I succeeded despite my language barrier and family struggles, I can overcome anything.
I want to go to a good college and make my father proud. I know he is always looking after me somewhere, encouraging me to continue studying as he had always taught me.
But I want my mother to be proud even more. Everyday she encourages me and makes sure I am on the right track. I want to succeed for her.
But that is not to say that I am trying to succeed merely for others. Most importantly, I want to succeed for myself. I have gone through some tough experiences, but that has not let me down. I want to achieve to my best ability so that I can be proud of myself.

My Future

I have no idea what I want to be in the future yet. But I still have time, and right now, I am constantly challenging myself to see what I am capable of doing.
However, I do have a few small dreams for the future. I want to travel around the world and see all the historical elements I have learned in the past few years. I also want a nice apartment in the city with enough space to put a grand piano. And a puppy.
